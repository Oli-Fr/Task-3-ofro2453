Concept Ideation

9th of August 2018 On this day, Kinson and I were paired together for the group assignment. Our first task was to examine each autonomous vehicular option and brainstorm as many possibilities as we could. We provided at least two viable options for each area. We looked into: Late night autonomous vehicle services with a focus on those under the influence, an autonomous vehicle service with a focus on tourism, and Drones that would allow businesses to examine crowds and manage more efficient flows throughout an area. Next we compiled a list of pros and cons for each potential idea. After waying down each option, we cut down all ideas into 3 main ideas we could pursue for our project. Towards the end of the tutorial, we decided it would be best if we do background research for each possible option, allowing us to eventually pick an option. We planned our first meet up for the coming Sunday where we make our final decision. {provide photo evidence of decision making} 12th of August 2018 During our first meetup, Kinson and decided to pursue autonomous transportation in the tourism industry. This was mainly due to our vast experiences with both tour services and ridesharing while overseas. Moreover, Kinson had visited many nations, where there was a clear language barrier, and felt it would be fitting for our joint venture. We felt our experiences would benefit the project. Finally we discussed what appropriate research methods we should be making use of. Ultimately {discuss reasoning behind why survey and interview, more personal bla bla} We agreed to write down questions for both survey and interview, so that next time in class, we could compile the best ones into separate documents.

Research

16th of August 2018 In the tutorial class, Kinson and I finalised the questions for our research methods. We made sure the more open ended questions would incorporated into our user surveys. That way, we could acquire a range of differing interpretations of the question. Questions that were more close ended were integrated into our online user survey. That day we began distributing our survey through Facebook and Wechat, to ensure we could collect data from a variety of different audiences. Moreover, we interviewed two students at University. One of which is international and the other a local student. {provide photo evidence of question we would ask} 19th of August 2018 On sunday, I spent most of the day going through all of the data and making general assumptions and collecting the initial takeaways. I then compiled data from the user survey and interviews into individual sticky notes which would eventually be built up into an affinity diagram. After letting the end result sink in, I started form patterns and see overall issues presented in transportation and tourism overseas. With that, I was able to begin writing my findings for assignment 1. {provide photo evidence of affinity diagramming}

Findings

20th of August 2018 On this day I began writing and designing the key elements for assignment 1. After finalizing my research questions, background information and reasoning for my research methods, I began working on my user personas. The personas reflected the two most common demographics of my research. Their personalities were derived from the main takeaways of the two demographic groups. I then worked on my story board. First I wrote down the general progression I was aiming for. After finalizing the steps, I started to pencil in the design for each individual frame. 21st of August 2018 With joint influence from the affinity diagram, personas and story board, I began work on the user journey map. The journey map would focus on the issues travellers face when using transportation to independently go sightseeing/participate in an activity while on holiday overseas. 22nd of August 2018 On the day before the assignment was due, I put all my focus on only implementing the key takeaways from my assignment and into a separate presentation I created. 25th of August 2018 Some time after the presentations, I started mapping out all the possibilities my project could venture towards.

To better orientate myself to the new project, I made the decision to analyse and make notes on all 3 of my group members’ assignments. That way, I could provide a new perspective to add onto the existing project. Next, the group showcased and explained app sketches. Medium Fidelity Prototype

13th of September 2018 For this week's class, my teammates and I mainly focused on the storyboarding and hand sketched wireframing for the assignment. At the start of the tutorial, we tested our initial hand sketched wireframe on two participants. With that, we were able draw out any shortcomings as well as the positive aspects to our design. Next, each member of the group took the initial design and worked on ways to improve it individually. After taking our time to work on the app design we came together and handed out our sketches to each group member. We wrote down what we liked and disliked as well as the advantages and disadvantages of distinct features. Next, we compiled our notes onto a shared document, which would be used when creating our final wireframe. On the document, we pasted links to similar applications or designs for inspiration. In the final hour of the tutorial, we examined the storyboards everyone had created for the previous assignment. With that, we decided upon a scenario that would theoretically be the most common task our service would undertake. As homework, I decided to create another storyboard that falls into line with our project. I decided upon how our service could taken advantage of in the paramedic/health services industry.
